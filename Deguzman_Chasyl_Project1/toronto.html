Some popular tourist attractions in Toronto include: CN Tower: A 1,815-foot-tall observation tower in downtown Toronto that offers panoramic views of the city. Ripley's Aquarium of Canada: A public aquarium located in downtown Toronto, home to many marine species and interactive exhibits. Royal Ontario Museum: A museum and art gallery featuring exhibits on natural history, world cultures, and art. Toronto Zoo: The largest zoo in Canada, home to over 5,000 animals and numerous interactive exhibits. Casa Loma: A historic castle and museum located in midtown Toronto.

Similarly to Montreal, Toronto is known for its diverse culinary scene and offers a wide range of dining options. Some popular local specialties include: Poutine: A Canadian dish made with fries, cheese curds, and gravy. BeaverTails: A fried dough pastry, often served with sweet toppings. Peameal bacon sandwich: A Canadian breakfast staple made with a cured, smoked, and wet-cornmeal-coated lean cut of back bacon. Butter tarts: A traditional Canadian pastry filled with a mixture of butter, sugar, and eggs.
